the immaculate foundation response to covid-19

We think of our local communities during the COVID-19 pandemic, and we would like to give all the support we can. Together with our partners, we are forming a network to step up and help our communities and lift the most vulnerable among us. We have been so inspired by the efforts put in by various individuals and organizations to help deal with this pandemic. We have 5 projects in the works, a small committed team of motivated youths and a growing number of donors, partners and well-wishers.

why we're here..

We want to create a platform where the African youth and the most vulnerable in society can access the skills and opportunities they need to succeed in the world tomorrow. We want to give them a new reason to smile because they can go to school, have a meal on their tables, a roof over their heads and access to quality healthcare. We believe that we can achieve this one step at a time, one pair of hands at a time: We believe that when we all come together, we create a better world.

Tabasamu

The activity at the Smile Community Center, Nairobi, is one of those small steps we are taking with a big ultimate goal. The objective is to provide food and other basic needs to help the children live better and safely through the pandemic and its associated difficulties. We want to channel resources for accomodation, pay school fees for the children and renovate the home. Over the course of 5 months, we're looking at spending just over KSH 100 000 with the help of partners, donors and well-wishers.

Project Managers - Boniface, Laura, David

Maria

A truly touching story, project Maria was a gesture to show that no amount of help is ever too little. The foundation set out with only the spirit of looking out for a neighbor, a small token and a big heart, and the hopes of giving Mama and her family a better day tomorrow and a reason to smile today.

Project Manager - Joy

Ujenzi

Among other projects under development, Ujenzi is an eye-opener to the livelihoods of the underprivileged and most vulnerable members of our society. Currently, we're focusing on the Smile Community center under project Tabasamu. The Children's home has a run-down drainage system, exposing its inhabitants to serious life-threatening health concerns, above the fact that they already are a risk group. We're looking forward to joining hands with the most inspired and touched individuals and groups to provide the children with a better place to stay, and lead healthier lives.

Project Managers - Boniface, Laura, David
